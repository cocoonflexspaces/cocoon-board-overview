How We Operate

What we commit to, on every booking, without exception.

These are not aspirational guidelines. They are standard operating procedure. Boards who have reviewed these commitments have found them to be straightforward and sufficient.

01

Zero cost and zero obligation to the building

No fee, no contract, and no financial obligation for the building or board. All arrangements are exclusively between Cocoon and the unit owner.

02

The unit owner approves every booking

Nothing is confirmed without the unit owner's explicit sign-off. The board is not involved in individual booking decisions — that responsibility sits entirely with the owner.

03

A Cocoon team member is on-site, always

The space is never left unattended. Our team manages client arrival, the booking itself, and close-out. For larger or more complex bookings, we may deploy two Casa Managers. We are the first and last people in the building each day.

04

Guest counts are limited and lists are confirmed

We set strict maximums for each booking type — typically 8–30 people, depending on the nature of the project. Every guest is confirmed by name before the booking date. There are no open invitations and no walk-ins.

05

No parties, no amplified music, no events with open bars

We do not accept social events or parties in residential buildings. If a booking doesn't fit the professional profile we require, we decline it — regardless of the fee.

06

Load-in and load-out within pre-approved windows

All equipment and materials move in and out during hours approved by the unit owner. We use freight entrances where available and coordinate directly with building staff as needed.

07

Certificate of Insurance naming the building on every booking

Every booking carries a COI that names the building as an additional insured, with waiver of subrogation. The board can request to see a sample COI at any time.

08

The building's address is never published or shared publicly

We do not list spaces by address, neighborhood, or any detail that would allow the public to identify the building. The address is shared only with confirmed, vetted clients under a signed agreement.

09

Residents are never approached or engaged without consent

Our clients interact only with the unit and Cocoon staff. We do not involve or contact other residents. Any neighbor who has questions or concerns is directed to the Casa Manager directly — not through building management or the unit owner.

10

Optional compensation arrangement for the board

For select bookings, we are open to discussing a compensation structure for the board. This is entirely at the board's discretion — there is no obligation to participate.

Questions Boards Typically Ask

Direct answers
to the right questions.

Who is liable if something goes wrong?

The renter. Every client is required to provide their own Certificate of Insurance, and our contract explicitly places liability with them. The building is named as an additional insured on every COI, with waiver of subrogation. Additionally, every booking is backed by a 25% security deposit that we hold and can apply against any claim. In practice, we have never needed to invoke it.

What does a booking actually look like from the building's perspective?

A small group of professionals arrives at a specific time, uses the unit, and leaves. Common areas see no more foot traffic than a typical dinner party. There is no signage, no music, no indication anything is happening.

How often does this happen?

On average, 1 to 2 bookings per quarter. There are quarters with no bookings at all. This is not a short-term rental operation — there is no daily turnover, no strangers in the hallway every weekend.

Who exactly is coming into the building?

Corporate professionals — media crews, fashion editors, brand representatives. Every client is vetted before we accept the booking. The unit owner sees the full client profile before approving.

Does anyone outside know this is happening?

No. We never publish the building's address. The location is shared only with confirmed clients, under a signed non-disclosure clause. There is no public listing associated with this building.

What if the board wants to try this before fully committing?

We offer a 6-month trial period for new building relationships. During that period, the board can observe how we operate and end the arrangement at any time, without explanation or penalty.

What about other residents in the building?

Neighbors are not affected or engaged in any way. If a neighbor happens to be interested in Cocoon's services for their own unit, we are happy to have that conversation separately — on their terms, entirely independently of this arrangement.

What does the board actually need to do?

Provide a simple letter of non-objection. That is the full extent of the board's involvement. Cocoon manages everything else — before, during, and after every booking.

A 6-month trial period is available.

We understand that any new arrangement in a residential building requires trust. We offer a structured 6-month trial — no long-term commitment, no obligation to continue. Most boards find it's enough time to see exactly how we work.
